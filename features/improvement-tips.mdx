AI Resume Analyzer doesn't stop at telling you what's wrong — it tells you exactly what to do about it. After evaluating your resume, the AI generates a prioritized set of improvement suggestions tailored to your specific content, along with curated course recommendations that address the skill gaps standing between you and your target role. Every tip is grounded in your actual resume, not generic advice, so the guidance you receive is relevant and immediately actionable.

## Types of Improvement Suggestions

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Wording & Clarity" icon="pen">
    Specific rewrites and phrasing suggestions that transform weak or passive bullet points into strong, impact-driven statements that communicate your value more effectively to both recruiters and ATS systems.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Missing Sections" icon="plus">
    Flags any resume sections that are absent but expected — such as a professional summary, LinkedIn profile URL, certifications block, or projects section — and explains why each one matters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keyword Optimization" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Highlights industry-relevant keywords and role-specific terms that your resume is missing, so you can add them strategically to improve both ATS ranking and recruiter relevance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Quantification" icon="chart-line">
    Identifies vague or generic statements in your experience section and prompts you to replace them with measurable achievements — numbers, percentages, timeframes, and dollar amounts that prove impact.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Course Recommendations

When your skill gap report identifies missing skills, the analyzer goes one step further by surfacing relevant online courses to help you close each gap. Recommendations are matched to the specific skills you're missing based on your target job description, so you're pointed toward learning that's directly useful rather than broadly related.

Suggested courses come from popular online learning platforms that offer both free and paid options, covering everything from technical certifications and programming languages to project management, data analysis, and soft-skills development. Each recommendation includes the skill it addresses, so you always know why a course is being suggested and how completing it will strengthen your candidacy.

You're never locked into a single course or provider — the recommendations are starting points, and you're free to use any equivalent resource that teaches the same skill.

## Acting on Feedback

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download or note your suggestions">
    At the end of your analysis, review all improvement suggestions in your report. Note which items are flagged as high priority, and keep a copy of the recommendations so you can work through them systematically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start with quick wins">
    Fix issues that take minutes, not hours — correct formatting inconsistencies, add a missing professional summary, fill in your LinkedIn URL, and sharpen vague bullet points. These small changes can meaningfully improve your score right away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enroll in recommended courses">
    For skill gaps that require actual learning, enroll in the suggested courses or equivalent resources. Even completing a short introductory course demonstrates initiative and gives you something concrete to add to your resume.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your resume">
    As you act on the suggestions — rewording bullets, adding sections, and gaining new skills — update your resume file to reflect each change. Keep a versioned copy so you can compare before and after.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-run the analysis">
    Upload your revised resume and run a fresh analysis. Compare your new score against your baseline to measure the impact of your changes and identify what still needs work.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Course recommendations are curated suggestions based on your identified skill gaps and are provided for informational purposes only. AI Resume Analyzer is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or partnered with any specific course provider or learning platform.
</Info>
