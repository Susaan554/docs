## Understanding Your Resume Score

Your resume receives an overall score that gives you a single, at-a-glance measure of how job-ready it is. The score is calculated across three main factors:

- **Content quality** — Are your bullet points specific, achievement-oriented, and free of filler language? The AI evaluates the strength and clarity of how you describe your experience.
- **Completeness** — Does your resume include all the sections a recruiter expects to see, such as a professional summary, work experience, education, skills, and contact information?
- **ATS compatibility** — Can an Applicant Tracking System parse your resume correctly? Formatting choices, keyword density, and section labeling all affect this component of your score.

A higher score means your resume is more likely to pass automated filters and impress human reviewers. Use the score as a baseline, then re-analyze after making changes to track your progress.

## ATS Optimization

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that most companies use to automatically screen resumes before a human recruiter ever sees them. If your resume isn't formatted and worded in a way the ATS can read, it may be filtered out — even if you're a strong candidate.

To improve your ATS score, keep these practices in mind:

- **Use standard section headings** such as "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills" rather than creative alternatives that an ATS may not recognize.
- **Avoid tables, text boxes, and graphics** — most ATS engines cannot parse content inside these elements and will skip that information entirely.
- **Incorporate keywords from the job description** directly into your experience bullets and skills section, using the same phrasing the employer uses.
- **Save and submit your resume as a plain `.pdf` or `.docx`** file unless the application specifies otherwise.
- **Spell out acronyms at least once**, since an ATS may search for either the full term or the abbreviation.

<Tip>
  Tailor your resume for each application by including keywords from the job description. Even small changes — swapping a synonym for the exact term used in the posting — can meaningfully improve how an ATS ranks your resume.
</Tip>
