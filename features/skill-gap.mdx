Skill gap analysis compares what your resume demonstrates against what a specific job requires, then surfaces the difference in plain terms. Instead of guessing why you're not hearing back from applications, you get a concrete list of missing skills and a starting point for closing each one. Whether you're pivoting industries, targeting a promotion, or refreshing a resume after a career break, understanding your gaps is the first step toward filling them.

## What Skills Are Detected?

AI Resume Analyzer identifies skills across six categories, giving you a broad picture of your professional profile rather than focusing on technical abilities alone.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Technical Skills" icon="code">
    Programming languages, scripting, data analysis, software engineering, and other hard technical competencies found in your resume or required by the job description.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Frameworks & Libraries" icon="layer-group">
    Specific frameworks, libraries, and development tools such as React, TensorFlow, Spring Boot, or Pandas — skills that are frequently called out explicitly in job postings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tools & Platforms" icon="wrench">
    Software, cloud platforms, productivity tools, and industry-specific platforms like AWS, Salesforce, Figma, or Jira that employers expect candidates to know.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Soft Skills" icon="people-group">
    Communication, leadership, collaboration, problem-solving, and other interpersonal competencies that appear in job descriptions and can be evidenced in your experience bullets.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Certifications" icon="certificate">
    Professional certifications and credentials — such as AWS Certified Solutions Architect, PMP, or Google Analytics — that are listed as requirements or preferred qualifications.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Domain Knowledge" icon="building">
    Industry-specific knowledge areas such as healthcare regulations, financial modeling, supply chain operations, or digital marketing strategy that signal sector fluency.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Use Your Skill Gap Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify your highest-priority gaps">
    Review the missing skills list and focus first on skills that appear as required (not just preferred) in the job description. These are the gaps most likely to disqualify your application if left unaddressed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the recommended courses">
    For each identified gap, the analyzer surfaces relevant learning resources to help you build that skill. Browse the recommendations and pick courses that fit your timeline and learning style.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add skills as you acquire them">
    Once you've completed a course or gained hands-on experience with a skill, update your resume to reflect it. Add it to your skills section and, where possible, include a bullet point that demonstrates how you applied it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-analyze to track improvement">
    Upload your updated resume and run the analysis again. Your new score and updated gap report will show you exactly how much ground you've gained and what still needs attention.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Re-Analyzing After Upskilling

Your skill gap report is not a one-time snapshot — it's a tool you can return to as your resume evolves. Any time you complete a course, earn a certification, finish a new project, or update your resume with fresh experience, re-upload it to get a current analysis. The AI will re-evaluate your resume from scratch, giving you an up-to-date score, a revised gap list, and refreshed recommendations that reflect your progress. There's no limit to how many times you can run an analysis, so treat it as an ongoing part of your job search workflow.

<Tip>
  Focus on your top 3 missing skills first. Closing a few critical gaps has significantly more impact on your match score than spreading your effort across every item on the list at once.
</Tip>
