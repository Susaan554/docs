Job matching lets you measure how closely your resume aligns with a specific role before you apply. Paste any job description into AI Resume Analyzer alongside your resume and you'll receive a compatibility percentage that shows how well your current experience and skills map to what the employer is asking for. The higher the score, the stronger your application is likely to land.

## How Match Scoring Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Paste the job description">
    Copy the full job description from the posting and paste it into the job description field in the analyzer. Include the responsibilities, requirements, and preferred qualifications for the most accurate result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The AI extracts requirements">
    Gemini reads the job description and identifies the required skills, qualifications, experience levels, and keywords the employer is looking for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your resume is compared">
    The AI maps the skills and qualifications found in your resume against the requirements extracted from the job description, looking for direct matches, related experience, and gaps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A match percentage is calculated">
    Based on how many of the employer's requirements your resume satisfies, a compatibility score from 0–100% is generated and displayed in your report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gaps and suggestions are surfaced">
    Any skills or qualifications present in the job description but missing from your resume are listed, along with targeted suggestions for how to address them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Interpreting Your Match Score

<Accordion title="90–100%: Excellent Match">
  Your resume is strongly aligned with the job description. Your skills, experience, and keywords closely reflect what the employer is looking for. Submit your application with confidence — and make sure your cover letter reinforces your top qualifications to seal the impression.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="60–89%: Good Match">
  You're a solid candidate for this role, but there are some gaps worth addressing before you apply. Review the missing skills listed in your report and consider adding relevant experience, reframing existing bullet points to mirror the job description's language, or briefly noting transferable skills.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Below 60%: Needs Work">
  There are significant gaps between your current resume and what this role requires. Rather than applying immediately, use the skill gap report to understand which areas need the most attention. Prioritize closing the critical gaps through upskilling or reframing your experience, then re-analyze before submitting.
</Accordion>

## Tips for Higher Match Scores

Improving your match score is largely about ensuring your resume speaks the same language as the job description. Here are practical ways to close the gap:

- **Mirror the job description's exact phrasing** — if the posting says "stakeholder management," use that term rather than a synonym like "client relations."
- **Quantify your achievements** wherever possible — numbers, percentages, and dollar amounts make vague claims concrete and demonstrate real impact.
- **Move your most relevant experience to the top** of each role's bullet points so it's visible immediately, both to the AI and to recruiters.
- **Add a tailored skills section** that lists the tools, technologies, and competencies called out in the job description, as long as you genuinely have those skills.
- **Update your professional summary** to reflect the title and key requirements of the role you're targeting, signaling alignment from the very first line.

<Note>
  The match score is a guide, not a guarantee of interview success. Always tailor your application letter to complement your resume and speak directly to the specific needs of the role and company.
</Note>
