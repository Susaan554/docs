Job description matching is the most powerful feature in AI Resume Analyzer for targeted job applications. Instead of a general resume review, it compares your resume directly against a specific role and tells you exactly how well you align — and what's holding you back.

## What You Need

Before you run a job match analysis, have the following ready:

- **Your resume** — saved as a PDF or DOCX file (see [Uploading Your Resume](/guides/uploading-resume) for preparation tips)
- **The full job description** — the complete text of the posting you're targeting, including responsibilities, requirements, and preferred qualifications

## Running a Job Match Analysis

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your resume">
    Start by uploading your resume file. If you haven't done this before, follow the steps in the [Upload Resume guide](/guides/uploading-resume) to prepare and submit your file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Job Match analysis mode">
    Once your file is loaded, choose **Job Match** from the analysis mode options. This activates the job description input field and enables compatibility scoring.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the complete job description">
    Copy the full job description from the job posting and paste it into the text field. Include all sections — responsibilities, required qualifications, and preferred qualifications. The more complete the description, the more accurate your match score will be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Analyze">
    Click the **Analyze** button to start the job match analysis. The AI will compare your resume against the job description and calculate your compatibility score.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your match score and missing skills">
    When the report loads, check your **Match Score** at the top, then scroll through the **Skill Gaps** section to see exactly which skills from the job description are absent or underrepresented in your resume.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Getting the Best Results

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use the Full JD" icon="file-lines">
    Don't summarize or paraphrase the job description. Paste the entire original text to give the AI the full context it needs for the highest-accuracy match.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Target One Role at a Time" icon="bullseye">
    Analyze one job posting per session. Focusing on a single role produces specific, actionable feedback rather than generic suggestions spread across multiple positions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Check Multiple Roles" icon="layer-group">
    Run separate analyses for different job postings to discover which roles your current resume aligns with most strongly — helpful when you're exploring options.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Iterate Quickly" icon="arrows-rotate">
    After receiving feedback, update your resume to address the identified gaps, then re-run the analysis. Watch your match score climb with each revision.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Understanding the Results

Your job match report breaks down exactly where your resume succeeds and where it falls short relative to the target role. You'll see a compatibility percentage, a prioritized list of missing skills, and tailored recommendations to close those gaps. For a full walkthrough of every report section and how to act on the findings, see the [Reading Your Report guide](/guides/reading-your-report) and the [Job Matching feature reference](/features/job-matching).

<Note>
  Paste plain text from the job description. Avoid copying rich text directly from formatted documents or applications — special characters and hidden formatting can interfere with the AI's ability to parse the content accurately.
</Note>
